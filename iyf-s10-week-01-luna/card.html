sunsets

Winter and fall sunsets are often more vibrant than summer sunsets because the air is less humid and contains fewer pollutants.

The colors of a sunset are caused by the scattering of light by the atmosphere. When the sun is low on the horizon, its light has to pass through more of the Earth's atmosphere, which scatters the shorter blue and green wavelengths and allows the longer red and orange wavelengths to dominate, creating the beautiful colors we see in a sunset.
